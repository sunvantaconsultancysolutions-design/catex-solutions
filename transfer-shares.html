FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the procedure for share transfer?

Share transfer in a private company involves executing Form SH-4, paying necessary stamp duty, submitting the form to the company along with share certificates, obtaining board approval via a resolution, updating the Register of Members, and endorsing/re-issuing the share certificates.

2. What stamp duty is applicable?

Stamp duty on the transfer of shares is 0.25% of the consideration value (the sale price of the shares). Share transfer stamps must be affixed to the SH-4 form and cancelled properly.

3. Are there restrictions on share transfers in a Private Company?

Yes, by definition, the Articles of Association (AOA) of a Private Limited Company restrict the right to transfer shares. Typically, existing shareholders have a pre-emptive right of first refusal before shares can be sold to an outsider.
