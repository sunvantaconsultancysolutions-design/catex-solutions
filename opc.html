Frequently Asked Questions

Only a natural person who is an Indian citizen and resident in India can form an OPC. NRIs cannot form an OPC. The individual must not be a minor.
An OPC must have one nominee who will take over in the absence of the sole member. The nominee must also be an Indian citizen and resident.
Yes, an OPC can be converted to a Private Limited Company voluntarily after 2 years or mandatorily when turnover exceeds ₹2 crore or paid-up capital exceeds ₹50 lakhs.
OPC must hold at least one Board Meeting, file MGT-7A (Annual Return), and AOC-4 (Financial Statements) annually.
