FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I close my OPC if it has never operated?

Yes, a dormant One Person Company (OPC) that has never commenced operations or has not carried on any business for a continuous period of at least one year can apply for closure under the abridged fast-track strike-off route.

2. What is the fast-track exit (FTE) route?

The fast-track exit (FTE) is a highly simplified winding-up mechanism. Instead of long liquidation procedures, the sole member files Form STK-2 with MCA along with stamp-duty paid affidavits and indemnity bonds, asking the ROC to strike off the OPC's name.

3. Are there any prior requirements before submitting Form STK-2?

Yes, before applying: the OPC must close its corporate bank account, pay off all liabilities, settle any pending government dues, prepare a nil statement of assets & liabilities certified by a Chartered Accountant, and obtain consents.
