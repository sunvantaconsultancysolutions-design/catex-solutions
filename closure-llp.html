FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How to close an LLP?

An LLP that has no assets or liabilities and has not been operating for at least one year can apply for direct strike-off by filing Form 24 with the Registrar of Companies (ROC), which requires the consent of all partners.

2. Can we close an LLP with pending annual filings?

No. Under current MCA guidelines, all pending annual returns (Form 11) and statement of accounts (Form 8) must be filed up to the date of cessation of business before applying for closure under Form 24.

3. What key documents are required to close an LLP?

The primary documents required are: consent of all partners, an affidavit from designated partners, an indemnity bond, a statement of assets & liabilities showing nil balances certified by a Chartered Accountant, and a bank account closure letter.
