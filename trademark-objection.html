FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Trademark Objection?

A Trademark Objection is raised by the Trademark Registrar during the examination of your trademark application. It is noted in an Examination Report and means the examiner has found reasons why your mark may not qualify for registration. You must reply within 30 days to keep your application alive.

2. What are common reasons for a TM objection?

Common reasons include: the mark being descriptive or non-distinctive, similarity to an already registered trademark, the mark being deceptive or contrary to public morality, and procedural deficiencies in the application form or documents.

3. What is the timeline to file a reply?

You have 30 days from the date of the Examination Report to file your reply. If no reply is filed within this time, the application is treated as abandoned. Our team ensures your reply is filed well within the deadline.

4. What happens if no reply is filed?

If no reply is filed within 30 days, the Trademark Registry will deem the application abandoned. You would then have to file a fresh trademark application and pay government fees again. It is critical to respond promptly and with strong legal arguments.
