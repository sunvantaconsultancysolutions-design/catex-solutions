Understanding Licensing

What is Trademark Licensing?

Trademark licensing is a contractual arrangement where the trademark owner (licensor) grants permission to another party (licensee) to use the trademark in connection with specific goods or services, in a defined territory, for a fixed duration — while the owner retains full ownership of the mark.

In India, a licensee is called a Registered User and must be recorded with the Trademark Registry via Form TM-U. Without this registration, the license may not be enforceable against third parties.

A well-drafted licensing agreement includes quality control provisions — ensuring the licensee maintains standards that protect the trademark's reputation and value. Royalty structures can be fixed fees, percentage of sales, or milestone-based payments.

Licensing allows trademark owners to expand their brand's reach, generate passive income, and enter new markets without direct operational involvement.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is trademark licensing?

Trademark licensing is a legal arrangement where the trademark owner (licensor) grants permission to another party (licensee) to use the trademark for agreed goods or services, in a specified territory and for a set period, in exchange for royalty or other consideration — while the owner retains all ownership rights.

2. What is a Registered User (TM-U)?

Under the Trade Marks Act 1999, a Registered User is a licensee whose right to use the trademark is formally recorded at the Trademark Registry via Form TM-U. This registration protects the licensee's rights against third parties and makes the license publicly known.

3. Can I license my trademark for only certain products or regions?

Yes. Trademark licenses can be restricted by territory (e.g., only Maharashtra), product category (e.g., only clothing), duration (e.g., 3 years), or even distribution channel. Partial licenses are fully valid and are often commercially preferred.

4. How is the royalty structure determined?

Royalties can be structured as a fixed annual fee, a percentage of net sales (typically 2–10%), milestone payments, or a combination. The structure depends on the brand's strength, market reach, and the licensee's business model. Our experts help you negotiate and draft a fair and enforceable royalty structure.
