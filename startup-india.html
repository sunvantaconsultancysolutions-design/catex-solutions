FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the Startup India scheme?

Startup India is a flagship initiative of the Government of India, launched in January 2016, to build a strong ecosystem for nurturing innovation and startups in India. DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) grants official recognition to qualifying startups, unlocking a range of tax and regulatory benefits.

2. What are the eligibility criteria?

To qualify for DPIIT recognition, your entity must: be incorporated as a Private Limited Company, LLP, or Partnership Firm; be less than 10 years old; have an annual turnover not exceeding ₹100 crore; be working towards innovation, development or improvement of products/processes/services; and have the potential to generate employment or create wealth.

3. What are the tax benefits for recognized startups?

DPIIT-recognized startups can apply for a 3-year income tax exemption under Section 80-IAC (for any 3 years out of the first 10 years). They are also exempt from Angel Tax (Section 56(2)(viib)) on investments received from recognized investors, reducing compliance burden significantly during early fundraising stages.

4. How long is the DPIIT recognition valid?

DPIIT recognition remains valid as long as the startup meets the eligibility criteria — i.e., the entity is not more than 10 years old and annual turnover has not exceeded ₹100 crore in any financial year since incorporation. There is no separate renewal process required for the recognition itself.
