FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Public Limited Company?

A Public Limited Company (PLC) is a company whose shares can be freely sold to the public and traded on stock exchanges. It requires a minimum of 7 shareholders and 3 directors. PLCs have more stringent regulatory compliance requirements but can raise large amounts of capital from the public.

2. What is the minimum capital required?

As per the Companies Act 2013 amendment, there is no minimum paid-up capital requirement for a Public Limited Company. However, for listing on stock exchanges, SEBI prescribes minimum net worth and other eligibility criteria that the company must meet.

3. How many directors are required?

A Public Limited Company requires a minimum of 3 directors and a maximum of 15 directors. Additionally, it must have at least one Independent Director and one Woman Director once certain thresholds are met. At least one director must be a resident of India.

4. Can a Public Limited Company do an IPO?

Yes, a Public Limited Company is the only type of company that can conduct an Initial Public Offering (IPO) and list its shares on stock exchanges like BSE or NSE. This makes it the preferred structure for businesses with long-term plans to raise public capital and scale significantly.
