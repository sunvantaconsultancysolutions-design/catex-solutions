FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What can be copyrighted?

Copyright protects original creative works including literary works (books, articles, software code), artistic works (paintings, photographs, sculptures), musical works (songs, compositions), dramatic works, films, and sound recordings. Ideas themselves cannot be copyrighted — only the expression of those ideas.

2. Is copyright registration mandatory in India?

No, copyright registration is not mandatory in India. Copyright exists automatically the moment an original work is created. However, registration provides legal evidence of your ownership, makes it easier to enforce your rights in court, and acts as a public record. It is highly recommended for serious creators and businesses.

3. How long does copyright protection last?

In India, copyright protection generally lasts for the lifetime of the author plus 60 years. For films, photographs, and anonymous/pseudonymous works, the protection is 60 years from the year of publication. After this period, the work enters the public domain.

4. What is the copyright registration process?

The process involves filing an application (Form XIV) with the Copyright Office, paying the government fee, and submitting copies of the work. A Diary Number is issued immediately as an acknowledgement. After a mandatory 30-day waiting period (for objections), the Copyright Office registers the work and issues the certificate.
