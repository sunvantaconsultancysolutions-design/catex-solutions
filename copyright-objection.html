FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a copyright objection?

A copyright objection is a formal notice issued by the Copyright Office or a third party challenging your copyright application. It may be raised by the Examiner (scrutiny objection) due to procedural deficiencies, or by a third party who claims prior rights in the work being registered.

2. What are common reasons for a copyright objection?

Common reasons include: incomplete or incorrect application details, third-party objections claiming similarity or prior creation of the work, lack of NOC from co-authors or employers, works falling in restricted categories, or procedural non-compliance. Each reason requires a specific, well-supported reply.

3. How long do I have to reply to an objection?

Upon receiving a copyright objection, you typically have 30 days to file a reply. Failure to respond within this period may result in abandonment of your copyright application. Our team prioritises deadline compliance and acts swiftly once you share the objection details.

4. What is the process after filing the reply?

After the reply is filed, the Copyright Office may either accept it and proceed with registration, or schedule a hearing where both parties present their case. Our experts attend the hearing on your behalf, present arguments, and follow up with the Copyright Office until a final decision is received.
