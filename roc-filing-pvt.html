FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What forms does a Pvt. Ltd. Company need to file annually?

A Private Limited Company must file two key annual forms: MGT-7 (Annual Return) containing shareholding pattern, director details, and company information; and AOC-4 (Financial Statements) containing balance sheet, profit & loss account, and auditor's report. Both forms are mandatory under the Companies Act 2013.

2. What are the due dates for ROC filing?

MGT-7 must be filed within 60 days from the conclusion of the Annual General Meeting (AGM). AOC-4 must be filed within 30 days from the AGM. The AGM itself must be held within 6 months of the financial year end (i.e., by September 30 for March financial year end). Our team keeps track of all your deadlines.

3. What is the penalty for late ROC filing?

Late filing attracts additional fees of ₹100 per day per form, with no upper cap. Continued non-compliance can result in strike-off of the company from the MCA register and disqualification of directors. Timely filing is critical to maintaining active company status.

4. What documents are needed for ROC filing?

You will need: audited financial statements (balance sheet, P&L), board resolution for approval of financials, list of shareholders and share transfers during the year, director details (DIN, PAN), MGT-9 extract (for companies above threshold), and DSC of the authorised director. Our team provides a detailed checklist upon engagement.
