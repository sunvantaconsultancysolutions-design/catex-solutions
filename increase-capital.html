FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the difference between authorized and paid-up capital?

Authorized capital is the maximum limit of shares a company is legally permitted to issue to shareholders, as registered in its Memorandum of Association (MOA). Paid-up capital is the actual amount of money received by the company from its shareholders when they purchase shares.

2. Can a company increase its authorized capital without shareholder approval?

No. Increasing authorized capital requires an alteration of the Memorandum of Association (MOA), which can only be done with the approval of shareholders by passing a resolution at an Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM).

3. What is the time limit to file Form SH-7 with the ROC?

Form SH-7 must be filed with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) within 30 days of passing the resolution in the shareholders' meeting. Delay in filing attracts an additional daily fee.
