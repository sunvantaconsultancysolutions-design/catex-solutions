FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why is a name search important before registration?

Choosing a name without checking availability can lead to MCA rejection during the SPICe+ form submission. A rejected name means delays and additional costs. Our search ensures your chosen name is unique, compliant with MCA naming rules, and has no trademark conflicts — saving you time and money.

2. What makes a company name unacceptable?

A name may be rejected if it is identical or too similar to an existing registered company, resembles a well-known trademark, includes restricted words (like "Bank", "Insurance", "Government") without approval, is offensive, or is purely generic/descriptive of the business activity. Our experts assess all these risk factors.

3. How can I check if a company name is available?

You can do a basic check on the MCA website, but it only shows exact matches. Our comprehensive service checks for phonetic similarities, trademark conflicts, and MCA naming guidelines — giving you a much more reliable picture of whether your chosen name is truly available and safe to use.

4. What should I do after the name search?

Once you have a confirmed available name, the next step is to proceed with company registration (Private Limited, LLP, OPC, etc.). Our team can seamlessly take you from name confirmation to full incorporation. We also recommend filing a trademark application alongside registration to protect your brand name.
