FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

1. When should I renew my trademark?

A trademark registration is valid for 10 years from the date of registration. You can apply for renewal from 6 months before the expiry date. We recommend filing early to avoid any lapses in protection for your brand.

2. Is there a grace period after expiry?

Yes. If you miss the renewal date, there is a grace period of 6 months during which you can still renew the trademark by paying an additional surcharge. It is always better to renew on time to avoid the extra fee.

3. What if the trademark has already expired?

If the trademark has expired and the grace period has also passed, the mark is removed from the register. You will then need to file a fresh trademark application. Act quickly — contact us to assess your situation and recommend the best course of action.

4. How do I renew my trademark?

The renewal is done by filing Form TM-R with the Trademark Registry along with the prescribed government fee. Our team handles the entire process — from checking the expiry date to filing the TM-R form and obtaining the renewed certificate on your behalf.
